Ahmed M. Hamza, Ph.D

Teaching Associate Professor · Department of Computer Science · University of Colorado Boulder

I work broadly within Applied AI and Computing Security areas of Computer Science; my background is in Security, Reinforcement Learning, and Digital Signal Processing (video and image encoding algorithms).

ahmed [[dot]] hamza \at/ colorado \dot\ edu

My interests sit at the intersection of AI and Computing Security. I try to answer questions like “How—and why—does this algorithm or model misbehave, what does it look like internally when it does, and what does that mean for other systems derived from it?”.

I also try to approach more general questions of capability in autonomous agentic applications, in areas like operational cybersecurity and system level reverse engineering.

My teaching is heavily within systems programming and adversarial system security + reverse engineering. I view Computing Security as a way to discover deep truths about the algorithms and systems we have built and continue to create.
